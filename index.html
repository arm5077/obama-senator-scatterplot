2000

During the Gore-Bush matchup, senators of both parties won frequently even when their state went for the other party in the presidential race.

What you're seeing: A big mush. Since senatorial and presidential results don't share much of a correlation, there's not much of a pattern to see.

2004

In 2004, that changed. States began lining up along ideological lines, and once-popular senators began feeling the heat of national campaigns in their own districts.

What you're seeing: The beginnings of a line forming from the bottom-left corner to the top right, showing a correlation forming.
